We have an approach that suggests a way forward. The confluence of principles of strategy and policy makes it possible to create and apply a framework that provides a new way of thinking about governance, and increases the possibility of creating solutions to what seem to be intractable problems for Portland and across the western United States. Why the University of Portland? Our university has embarked on a strategy of being not just the University of Portland, but the University of, for, and with Portland and its local, regional, national, and global communities. What better way to show that commitment than to collaborate on building solutions? It is easy to bemoan how Portland is discussed in the news — it is more efficacious to take the opportunity to be part of the solution, and to attract others, including students, who want to be part of it too. IN PROGRESS Unsheltered Homelessness in Portland — Mayor Wilson's Strategy Oliver Jeppe · University of Portland, Class of 2026 An analysis of Mayor Keith Wilson's strategy for reducing unsheltered homelessness in Portland. The paper examines how Wilson defines the challenge and the main policies he proposes to address it. It finds that Wilson's definition of the challenge is reasonable, but his proposed policies are unlikely to have a significant positive effect without changes. Read the paper IN PROGRESS Analyzing the Portland City Charter Commission's Governmental Structural Reform Strategy Maggie Dapp · University of Portland, Class of 2026 An examination of the recent reform to Portland city government — the problems the reform was meant to solve, and whether the reform was successful on its own terms. While it is difficult to draw clear conclusions so soon after implementation, this paper finds the reforms were partially successful at best, and that more clarity is needed on governance challenges in Portland and how to measure governance progress.
