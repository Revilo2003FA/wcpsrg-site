Unsheltered Homelessness in Portland — Mayor Wilson's Strategy

By Oliver Jeppe · University of Portland, Class of 2026 · 2026

Abstract

This paper is an analysis of Mayor Keith Wilson's strategy for reducing unsheltered homelessness in Portland. It explains how Wilson defines the challenge of homelessness in Portland and the main policies proposed to address that challenge. The main finding is that Wilson's definition of the challenge is reasonable, but his proposed policies are unlikely to have a significant positive effect on the challenge without changes.
